Deforestation tutorial

Hi! So you have decided to help us to find new deforested areas in the forest, Great!!

In the following steps you will learn how you can help us by simply marking deforested areas when you find them.

In this application you will get one tile that has been previously selected by you, the community, as the best satellite image for the area.

The goal: mark deforested areas if any.

In some tiles you will be able to identify clearly the brown areas that usually represent deforested areas like in the following photo.

In other cases it will be more complicated and you will have to take a decision.

Tip: Difficult tiles will usually have some comments, check the footer of the page to get more feedback.

When you find an area that you think it is deforested, you can mark it by using the Polygon tool that you can find in the black toolbar

Then, draw one or more polygons covering the area like in this image:

Tip: You can close the polygon by double clicking.

Info: Click in the Navigation button to browse the map again.

Sometimes you will find areas that you don't know if they are deforested or not, or areas that could be interesting to review by the experts.

In these cases, you can use the point tool

Then, click in the zone to place a blue marker that you think it is interesting for later review.
Info: Click in the Navigation button to browse the map again.

That's all!! But before you start marking deforested areas, you should know that we will show in some tiles orange, pink or blue areas representing respectively:

  • Orange: Indigenous area in the tile,
  • Pink: Conservation area in the tile,
  • Hydrography: Rivers, lakes, etc. in the tile.

The goal is to help you to distinguish water (sometimes look like deforested areas) and to know if the deforestation is happening in indigenous and/or conservation areas.

Info: You can disable them if you want for the current tile, by clicking in layers button.
